The CIER had sustained significant damage due to ocean plastics and synthetic contaminants and was completely rehabilitated ahead of the spring breeding and nesting season.

Rafael Point Flores Island Completed May 2018

Located in the heart of the Clayoquot Sound UNESCO World Biosphere Reserve and traditional Ahousaht First Nations territory, Rafael Point is a significant foraging, breeding and nesting area for a variety of wildlife. Orca, Gray and Humpback whales utilize waters surrounding Rafael Point as prime feeding grounds. Coastal wolves, migratory seabirds and pinnipeds utilize the area for foraging, breeding, and nesting/rookeries.

Vargas Island May/June 2018

Essential habitat for the wolf packs of Clayoquot Sound, Vargas Island provides important foraging and sanctuary havens for several unique packs which utilize the area year-round. Ongoing work includes rehabilitation of critical foraging habitat and pollution removal from inter-tidal zones frequently utilized by wolves and a multitude of other resident and migratory species.
